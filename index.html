01 — What it is

A transparent fact-checker for study notes.

Learners take notes quickly and miss subtle factual errors. TruthLens checks each claim against a trusted reference corpus and tells you whether it holds up — with the exact evidence sentence and a confidence, not a black box.

✂️

Claim by claim

Notes are split into individual sentences so each factual claim is judged on its own.

🔎

TF-IDF retrieval

Every claim is matched against the corpus by cosine similarity to pull the most relevant evidence.

⚖️

Three-way stance

A trained linear model rules each claim supported, contradicted, or not-enough-info.

🔗

Evidence + source

Each verdict shows the evidence sentence and its source label, never an unsourced assertion.

📐

Calibrated confidence

The classifier's probability becomes a confidence score on every verdict.

🧪

Tested & honest

15 pytest tests; ~0.77 held-out accuracy. No LLM — just retrieval and a linear classifier.

02 — How it works

Four steps from a note to a verdict.

Split into claims

The note is broken into sentences, keeping decimals and abbreviations intact.

Retrieve evidence

A TF-IDF vectorizer scores the corpus by cosine similarity and returns the best match.

Classify the stance

A logistic-regression model reads overlap, number-conflict, negation and antonym features.

Report the verdict

Supported, contradicted, or not-enough-info — with evidence, source, and confidence.

03 — Architecture

The retrieve-then-classify pipeline.

Each claim flows through a splitter, a TF-IDF retriever over the reference corpus, and a trained logistic-regression entailment model that emits the verdict. All in pure Python with scikit-learn.

  • scikit-learn
  • NumPy
  • SciPy

Retrieval

  • TfidfVectorizer

  • 9 lexical features
  • 98 seed triples
